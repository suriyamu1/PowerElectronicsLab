    How to choose the right components?

    Diode
    1. The voltage applied across the diode when it is reverse biased must always be lesser than the Peak Inverse Voltage (PIV) specified in the datasheet of the diode.
    2. The current through the diode (load current) must be lesser than the maximum anode current (Ia) specified in the datasheet of the diode.
    3. The current flows through the diode only when it is forward biased. The maximum value of current through diode Imax = Vpeak / Rload.
    4. For safe operation, Imax < Ia max. However the diode can withstand non-repetitive surge currents for a very short time.
    SCR
    • All the design aspects of diode are applicable to SCR.
    • Additionally, SCRs have another terminal (Gate).
    • The gate current applied to the SCR must not exceed the maximum gate current (IGM) specified in the datasheet
    • The power (voltage * current) applied to the gate terminal must not exceed the maximum gate power (PGM) specified in the datasheet
    • By increasing the gate current of the SCR, the forward voltage drop of the SCR can be reduced.
    Load resistance
    • In this virtual lab setup, we assume we have a rheostat (slider)
    • Current Rating of rheostat: (0 - 6) A
    • Power rating of rheostat: 10,000 W
    • Resistance Values: 10Ω to 1000Ω
    • The load resistance must be chosen such that the load current will be less than the maximum anode current of the SCRs and diodes.
    Voltmeter
    • The output voltage of a AC voltage controller is AC.
    • To measure AC voltage, Moving Iron Voltmeters are used. MI voltmeters give the rms value of the voltage.
    • To have higher accuracy, do not choose voltmeters with very high range than actually needed.
    • For example: To measure 450V rms AC, choosing 750V MI voltmeter will give inaccurate measurements. But choosing a 500V MI voltmeter will give accurate measurements.
    Ammeter
    • To measure AC current, MI Ammeters are used.
      MI ammeters give the rms value of the current.
    • To have higher accuracy, do not choose ammeters with very high range than actually needed.
    • For example: To measure 4A AC, choosing 7.5A MI ammeter will give inaccurate measurements. But choosing a 5A MI ammeter will give accurate measurements.
