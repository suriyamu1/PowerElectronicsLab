    • What is a Chopper?

    • A chopper is a static device which converts fixed input voltage to variable output voltage.
    • A chopper can be thought as a DC equivalent of an AC transformer as it steps-down or steps-up DC voltage.
    • The switching device used for a chopper circuit can be power BJT, power MOSFET, GTO or force-commutated thyristors (commutation circuit is needed if thyristors are used).
    • There are 2 types of choppers, Step-down chopper and step-up chopper. (Step-down chopper is more common)

    Simple Step Down Chopper (with resistive load)

    • A chopper is a high speed on/off semiconductor switch. It connects source to load and disconnects the load from source at a fast speed.
    • These semiconductor switching devices can be generally represented by a switch (sw) with an arrow as shown.
    • When the switch is on, current flows in the direction of arrow. When the switch is off, no current can flow.
    • In this manner, a chopped load voltage (Vo) as shown, is obtained from a constant DC supply of magnitude (Vs)

    Operating Principle of Step-down Chopper

    • The switch is being operated with a duty cycle D
    • For a constant frequency, the duty cycle is defined as the ratio of the switch on time to the sum of switch on and switch off times.
    • Where T is the time period. T = 1/f (f is the switching frequency)
    • The average value of the output voltage Vo = D * Vs
    • The duty cycle 0 <= D <= 1
    • Hence the output (avg) voltage is always less than the input voltage. That is why this chopper is called as step-down chopper.
    • In resistive loaad, When sw is off, Load current becomes zero
    • In RL or RLE load, when sw is off, the inductor does not allows the load current to become zero. The inductor supplies current and it flows via the freewheeling diode.. causing the voltage across load (Vo = Vdiode) to be zero

    Need for a freewheeling diode in chopper circuit

    • It is an anti-parallel diode connected across the load to provide a path for a continuous inductor current flow when the switch is in off state
    • A freewheeling diode ensures continuous conduction mode if the load current never reaches zero and the load time constant (tau) = L/R is much greater than the period T.

    • The step-down chopper provides positive voltage and positive current at all times. So this is a first quadrant chopper

    Additional Theory

    Advantages of DC Chopper
    • HIGH OPERATING FREQUENCIES
    • Choppers can operate at high switching frequencies (KHz to MHz). The higher the operating frequency, the smaller and lighter the transformers, filter inductor and capacitors.
    • High operating frequencies allow for achieving a faster dynamic response to rapid changes in the load current and/or in input voltage.

    • Low component count
    • Choppers can achieve high conversion efficiency
    • REASON: Because there is very small power loss
    • In off state, zero current through switch -> P = V*I = V*0 = 0
    • In on state, very low voltage across the switch (in the range 0.6V) so P = V*I P is low
    • Relatively simple control (stepless variation of output voltage by controlling duty cycle of semiconductor switch)
    Disadvantages of DC Chopper
    • PWM rectangular voltage and current waveforms can cause turn-on and turn-off losses in semiconductor devices, which limit their practical operating frequencies to the range of 100 KHz.
    • Rectangular waveforms generate Electromagnetic Interference (EMI)
    Modes of Conduction
    • The DC-DC converters can operate in two distinct modes with respect to the inductor current


    • 1. Continuous Conduction Mode (CCM)
    • The state of the converter in which the inductor current is never zero for any period of time is called the continuous conduction mode (CCM).
    • When the switch sw is off, the freewheeling diode conducts to support an uninterrupted current in the inductor.
    • When the switch sw is on, the freewheeling diode is reverse biased.
    • CCM is prefered for high efficiency and good utilization of semiconductor switches and passive components.


    • 2. Discontinuous Conduction Mode (DCM)
    • When the average value of the output current is low (high R) and/or the switching frequency f is low, the converter may enter the discontinuous conduction mode (DCM)
    • In DCM, the inductor current is zero during the portion of the switching period.

    • For a buck converter, the value of the filter inductance that determines the boundary between CCM and DCM is given by Lb = (1-D)R/2f

    Applications of DC-DC converters
    • Step-down choppers are most used in high-performance dc drives, for example, electric traction, electric vehicles, etc
    • Step-up choppers are primarily used in radar and ignition systems.
    • The dc-dc converters are building blocks of distributed power supply systems in which a common dc bus voltage is converted to various other votlages according to requirements of particular loads. Such distributed systems are common in computer and telecommunication equipment.
    • Buck boost converters are used in dedicated battery chargers.
    • Boost converters are used in power factor correction applications.
